<p class="p1">
  I spent an afternoon at my local <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Harold_Washington_Library"><span class="s1">public library</span></a> (check out the ninth floor winter garden if you’re ever in downtown Chicago!), and came across a book published in 1987 titled <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Soviet-Commercial-Design-Mikhail-Anikst/dp/1558591524"><span class="s1">Soviet Commercial Design of the 20’s</span></a>. Amongst a myriad of Soviet propaganda-leaning gems was a series of posters by Dmitry Bulanov. The internet yielded little information on <a href="http://www.rusartnet.com/biographies/russian-artists/20th-century/modern/soviet/poster-design/dmitry-bulanov"><span class="s1">Bulanov’s training and history</span></a>, but his illustration style and treatment of Cyrillic characters is really refreshing.
</p>
