Fonts and written texts are the most important thing when you visit a webpage. Even though some people aren&rsquo;t always likely to read a long text, the lack of written texts makes the page look empty of message.

No matter if you uploaded hundreds photos: you should still add a few words, like descriptions or so, to attract people&rsquo;s attention. Otherwise your page will look as a place with a bunch of colors, shapes that anyone can give his own meaning, according to their cultural background.

Many times [photographers want to watermarks][1] their photos. Is that really necessary?

## Watermarks: yes or no?

It&rsquo;s a very common question that at least once in their life photographers had to answer. Is watermark a sign of professionalism? Probably a watermark can make you feel more professional, but poorly designed watermarks could make you look as a simple amateur.

You should then choose a very elegant and refined font in order to convey a sense of professional work.

More often, a watermarked photo can look commercial and probably this is not the effect on people that&nbsp; you would like to achieve!

Maybe a watermark protects your works from being downloaded and stolen. So you should place your watermark in a strategic position into the frame of the photo, so that if someone try to remove your watermark no post production programs would adjust the photos as nothing were.
